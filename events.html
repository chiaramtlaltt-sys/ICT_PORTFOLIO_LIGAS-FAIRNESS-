School Events!

Division Meet <𝟑ִֶָ𓂃 ࣪˖ ִֶָ🏐་༘࿐

"Winners never quit and quitters never win." — Vince Lombardi

Sports is not just a competition of strength, but a competition of wit, commitment, and teamwork. Being able to witness the talent and fruit of the hardwork of many athletes during the division meet taught me this. They say people either rise as a winner or leave a loser, but I believe that's false. I believe it's a win in itself to be able to represent a your school or an entire cluster. I believe it's a win in itself to be able to perform your best on that fateful day. Whether it be basketball, volleyball, or dancesport, every athlete never quit, that's why they are winners.

It was truly insipiring to see how amazing these people were able to perform. You really learn to never give up, because one day, when you are standing in front of a cheering crowd, you will know it was truly worth it. I doesn't matter how strong or skillful you start as, what matters is the spirit to learn and grow.

English month ᝰ🖋️ˎˊ˗.𖥔 ݁ ˖

"To be or not to be, that is the question." — William Shakespeare

English month celebrates one thing most of us grew up on; stories. Whether it be folk tales or children's fiction, a child who grew up on imagination is a child that is creative and free. Literature and english helps us express ourselves through emotional poetry, interesting essays, and lively stories. This is one of the peak art forms of human expression, going back to the prehistoric times. We created stories to understand, to express, and to entertain.

This month, as words on a page come into life through the students' book character portrayal, we learn just how important writing is to humans.

Values month 𐙚⋆°｡⋆♡

"Alone we can do so little, together we can do so much" -Helen Keller

I believe Values Month is truly the peak of an LPSCI school year.

Whenever December rolls around, I can always expect the chatter of the students saying "Huy! VPOP season nanaman! Tsaka yung MVP! Sino kaya rep natin?" This is when you expect sections and batches to be competitive again. But even when the competition gets fierce, we should never forget the meaning of values month; to celebrate and show the values that make us a good Lapiscian.

Through VPOP, we are able to showcase our talents in songwriting and of course, dancing! As a section performance, it gets hard sometimes to coordinate. But this event brings us together in a special way. I'll never forget the nervous "Goodluck!" and "Let's do this!" before the actual performance. But in the end, we exit the stage with a smile as we congratulate ourselves for being able to perform sufficiently.

Values month truly lives up to its meaning. Through its various activities, we not only learn to appreciate the values we also have, but grow and nuture more values to be shared with our peers.

Christmas Party ·꙳⋆𖢔𖠰𖢔·꙳⋆

"And may the spirit of Christmas be always in our hearts" -Jose Mari Chan

After seeing Christmas decorations in malls up in September and hearing the same song for four months, it is finally Christmas season. The Christmas party is one of the most awaited celebrations of the year (not only because of the break right after.) We gather to celebrate the holidays, Christmas, Hanukkah, and a lot more. This event helped bring me closer to my current class. I am happy to close the 2025 part of the school year off with them. It's not just about the gifts, it's celebrating the spirit of Christ's birth and sharing love and peace with the people around us.

Math month + × ÷ -

"Without mathematics, there's nothing you can do. Everything around you is mathematics. Everything around you is numbers." — Shakuntala Devi

Without knowing it, we use mathematics everyday. To calculate how much change you'll get, to see how many minutes remain until the end of class, or to estimate how much cash you would need to commute home. Though not everyone may like it, it is pretty essential to our day-to-day lives. This month specifically celebrates that through singing songs of math, expressing our skill in murals of math, decorating a garden, and explaining life through mathematical terms. These may be pretty mundane, but it really highlights the use of mathematics in pretty much everything!

MAPEH month ⊹₊⟡⋆🎨🖌️⋆⟡₊⊹

"Wholesome exercise in the free air, under the wide sky, is the best medicine for body and spirit." — Sarah Louise Arnold

MAPEH is one of those subjects that expand onto everything. This month helps us hone our talents in music and arts while also promoting our physical health. MAPEH is not just about physical activity, it's also about culture, tradition, and history. The folk dance competition brings us back to our Filipino roots through showcasing how our ancestors performed ceremonial or leisurely dances. The arts competition helps young artists learn the many components of arts.

Behind all the competition, we learn to appreciate the arts of being human.

SSLG elections 🗣

"A leader takes people where they want to go. A great leader takes people where they don't necessarily want to go, but ought to be." — Rosalynn Carter

These elections tell us how to act when we become eligible voters in the future. Student council leaders are often examined down to the core. They should be a good, true leader who wants to help the school. They should not be someone who ran just for popularity and influence. This should be the same way we treat the people who run for the government as well. They not only dictate what happens at school anymore, they influence the entire country now, all Filipinos. So if we want a good future as a proper voter, we should start with the SSLG. Listen to their speeches, hear their answers, and analyze their way of campaigning, and remember to do the same in the future.
